A differential cut dC adds formula R to the evolution domain Q of a differential equation system [{x′=f(x) & Q}]P, so that the resulting differential equation system has the form [{x′=f(x) & Q∧R}]P.

The cut R may refer to the initial state of the differential equation system using the special function symbol old(.), e.g., x=old(x) + vt.

See also

  • Differential invariant dI to prove the formula R is an invariant of the differential equation system.
  • Differential weakening dW to prove the postcondition P from only the evolution domain Q∧R after sufficient strengthening with differential cuts dC.
